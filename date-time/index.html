2 Daylight Saving Time (DST)

Spring Forward

Clocks move ahead 1 hour (e.g., 2 AM ‚Üí 3 AM). Day is 23 hours. US: 2nd Sunday of March.

Fall Back

Clocks move back 1 hour (e.g., 2 AM ‚Üí 1 AM). Day is 25 hours. US: 1st Sunday of November.

Not all places observe DST: Most of Africa (including Nigeria), most of Asia, and parts of the US (Arizona, Hawaii) don't use DST. Always check both the zone AND the DST status.
Worked Example 2
Scheduling Across DST Changes
Problem: You schedule a weekly call at 2:00 PM New York time with a colleague in London. In winter, NY is EST (UTC‚àí5) and London is GMT (UTC+0). What happens when the US springs forward but the UK hasn't yet? (US DST starts ~2 weeks before UK)
Before DST
NY 2:00 PM EST (UTC‚àí5) = London 7:00 PM GMT (UTC+0)
Offset = 5 hours
During the gap (US springs forward, UK hasn't)
NY 2:00 PM EDT (UTC‚àí4) = London 6:00 PM GMT
Offset shrinks to 4 hours ‚Äî call is 1 hour earlier for London!
Answer: The London colleague sees the meeting shift from 7 PM to 6 PM for ~2 weeks. This "DST gap" causes real-world scheduling chaos every spring. Best practice: schedule in UTC or use calendar tools that handle time zones automatically.

3 The Y2038 Problem

Many systems store Unix time as a signed 32-bit integer, which maxes out at 2,147,483,647 ‚Äî January 19, 2038 at 03:14:07 UTC. After that, it overflows to a negative number (December 13, 1901).

Real impact: Embedded systems, IoT devices, legacy databases, and some file formats still use 32-bit timestamps. This is why the tech industry is actively migrating to 64-bit time.

Business Days ‚Äî Working Day Calculations & Scheduling

Counting workdays, excluding weekends and holidays ‚Äî essential for project deadlines, shipping estimates, and legal timelines.

1 Counting Business Days

A business day is typically Monday‚ÄìFriday, excluding public holidays. Different countries have different holiday calendars.

Business days ‚âà Total days √ó (5/7) ‚àí Holidays
Quick estimate: ~21.7 business days per month~260 business days per year (before holidays)~250 after US federal holidays

2 The Full-Weeks Method

For exact business day counts: separate full weeks (which always have 5 business days) from the remaining partial week.

Business days = (full weeks √ó 5) + extra weekdays
Full weeks = total days √∑ 7 (integer part)Extra days = total days mod 7 (check each one)
Worked Example 1
Business Days Between Two Dates
Problem: How many business days between Monday, January 6 and Friday, February 14, 2025? (No holidays in this range.)
Step 1: Total calendar days
Jan: 31 ‚àí 6 = 25 days remaining
Feb: 14 days
Total = 25 + 14 = 39 calendar days
Step 2: Full weeks and remainder
39 √∑ 7 = 5 full weeks + 4 extra days
Full weeks: 5 √ó 5 = 25 business days
Step 3: Check the 4 extra days
Start from Monday: Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu
All are weekdays ‚Üí 4 business days
Total = 25 + 4 = 29 business days
Answer: 29 business days. The full-weeks method is reliable and fast ‚Äî separate the complete weeks (always 5 workdays) and handle the partial week manually.
Worked Example 2
Deadline: "10 Business Days From Today"
Problem: A document is due in 10 business days from Wednesday, March 5, 2025. What is the deadline? (No holidays.)
Step 1: Full weeks in 10 business days
10 √∑ 5 = 2 full weeks = 14 calendar days
Step 2: Add to start date
March 5 (Wed) + 14 calendar days
= March 19, 2025 (Wednesday)
Answer: Wednesday, March 19, 2025. When business days divide evenly by 5, you land on the same weekday. 10 business days = exactly 2 calendar weeks. If it were 8 business days: 1 week (5 days) + 3 more weekdays = Thu, Fri, Mon ‚Üí March 17 (Monday).
Worked Example 3
Project Duration with Holidays
Problem: A task needs 15 business days starting Monday, December 16, 2024. Christmas (Dec 25) and New Year's Day (Jan 1) are holidays. When does it end?
Step 1: Count week by week
Week 1 (Dec 16‚Äì20): 5 business days ‚úì
Week 2 (Dec 23‚Äì27): 4 days (Dec 25 = holiday)
Week 3 (Dec 30‚ÄìJan 3): 4 days (Jan 1 = holiday)

Step 2: Remaining days

Answer: January 7, 2025 (Tuesday). Without holidays, 15 business days = 3 weeks = Jan 3. The 2 holidays pushed it 2 business days later. Always account for holidays in project timelines!
